Department of Zoology

Welcome to the Zoology department, a hub for exploring the fascinating world of animals, their ecosystems, and their evolutionary relationships.

About Zoology

Zoology is a branch of biology that delves into the study of animals, their behaviors, classifications, and interactions with the environment. The department equips students with knowledge and skills for advanced research and application in ecological and biological sciences.

Level 100 - Zoology

An introduction to animal biology, covering basic concepts of evolution, physiology, and ecological systems.

Courses at Level 100

Students at Level 100 explore foundational topics in Zoology, including animal diversity, basic genetics, and evolutionary principles. This level lays the groundwork for advanced studies in Zoology.

Back to Zoology

Level 200 - Zoology

A deeper dive into the anatomy, physiology, and taxonomy of animal species.

Courses at Level 200

Level 200 focuses on the biological processes that sustain life, animal classification, and in-depth studies of vertebrate and invertebrate anatomy. Students gain a comprehensive understanding of zoological systems.

Back to Zoology

Level 300 - Zoology

Advanced studies in evolutionary biology, genetics, and ecology, with hands-on fieldwork.

Courses at Level 300

At Level 300, students engage in advanced courses in genetics, animal behavior, and evolutionary biology. The level includes practical sessions in ecosystem analysis and wildlife management.

Back to Zoology

Level 400 - Zoology

Final year students undertake research projects and specialize in fields like marine biology, conservation, or wildlife ecology.

Courses at Level 400

Level 400 is focused on independent research, advanced studies in biodiversity, and conservation strategies. Students develop specialized skills for professional or academic careers in Zoology.

Level 100 - Chemistry

Introduction to Chemistry: Explore atomic structures, chemical bonds, and basic reactions.

About Level 100 Chemistry

Students in Level 100 Chemistry begin their journey by learning the foundational concepts of atomic structure, periodicity, bonding, and stoichiometry. These courses prepare students for advanced topics in higher levels.

Key Topics:

  • Introduction to the Periodic Table
  • Atomic and Molecular Structures
  • Chemical Bonding
  • Balancing Chemical Equations

Example Note:

Periodic Table Overview: The periodic table organizes elements based on atomic number, electron configurations, and recurring chemical properties.

Back to chemistry

Level 200 - Chemistry

Dive deeper into organic chemistry, thermodynamics, and reaction mechanisms.

About Level 200 Chemistry

Level 200 Chemistry builds upon foundational knowledge by introducing intermediate topics like thermodynamics, reaction mechanisms, and the principles of organic and inorganic chemistry.

Key Topics:

  • Thermodynamics and Heat Transfer
  • Introduction to Organic Chemistry
  • Reaction Mechanisms
  • Laboratory Practices and Safety

Example Note:

**Thermodynamics**: Study the principles governing energy changes in chemical reactions, such as enthalpy, entropy, and Gibbs free energy.

Back to Chemistry

Level 300 - Chemistry

Advanced studies in chemical kinetics, spectroscopy, and analytical techniques.

About Level 300 Chemistry

Level 300 Chemistry focuses on advanced topics like kinetics, spectroscopy, and analytical chemistry. These courses emphasize problem-solving and experimental techniques.

Key Topics:

  • Chemical Kinetics
  • Spectroscopy (UV-Vis, IR, NMR)
  • Analytical Chemistry Techniques
  • Environmental Chemistry

Example Note:

**Chemical Kinetics**: Kinetics examines reaction rates and the factors influencing them, such as concentration, temperature, and catalysts.

Back to Chemistry

Level 400 - Chemistry

Specialized topics and research projects in modern chemistry.

About Level 400 Chemistry

Level 400 Chemistry prepares students for professional or academic careers by focusing on research, specialized fields like computational chemistry, and capstone projects.

Key Topics:

  • Computational Chemistry
  • Advanced Organic Synthesis
  • Nanotechnology and Materials Chemistry
  • Capstone Research Projects

Example Note:

**Nanotechnology in Chemistry**: This field explores materials and processes at the nanometer scale, enabling breakthroughs in drug delivery, energy storage, and more.
